playerlab

Six ways the now-playing player can merge into the profile header instead of floating as the bottom dock. Every option keeps the non-negotiables: the play/pause is the scrubber knob, and heart/seen stay one tap away. Web layout first; each collapses cleanly on mobile.

Option 1

Status line — the Last.fm move

A slim listening strip fused to the hero's bottom edge. The header stays untouched; the strip appears only while playing. Cheapest to build, quietest to look at — the profile reads "this person is listening right now" like a status.

Option 2

Hero B-side — split card

The hero becomes a two-panel card: identity on the left, a proper now-playing panel on the right with artwork and the knob-scrubber. Feels like the player belongs to the profile, not on top of it. Costs the most horizontal room; panel hides when idle.

Option 3

Ribbon cell — a fifth stat

Now-playing joins the stat ribbon as a wider cell: EQ bars, title, knob. Reads as "listening" being part of your scene stats — nights, venues, seen live… and what's on right now. Tightest integration with zero new bands; the cell is a taste-signal on public profiles too.

Option 4

Backdrop takeover — the header goes on air

While playing, the set's artwork becomes a dimmed backdrop for the whole hero and a scrub rail docks to its bottom edge with an ON AIR tag. Most atmospheric — the profile visibly changes state — with the CRT scanlines doing the brand work. Needs careful contrast on busy thumbnails.

Option 5

Turntable chip — the record on the avatar

A little spinning record docks onto the avatar (it IS the play/pause), and a one-line "Listening to…" caption sits under the handle. Smallest footprint of all six, very collectible-feeling — pairs naturally with the In-rotation record rail below. Scrubbing moves to a hover/expand state.

Option 6

Marquee ticker — CRT crawler above the card

A scrolling NOW PLAYING ticker caps the hero like a broadcast chyron, with a compact knob-scrubber pinned right. Leans hardest into the Sunset·CRT identity (it's basically the arcade attract-screen). Loudest of the six — great if the profile should feel like a station.

All six are states of the SAME dock player — the bottom dock stays everywhere else in the app; on the profile page it hands off to the chosen header treatment while something is playing. 1 and 5 are the cheapest to ship; 2 is the most "player-forward"; 3 treats listening as a stat; 4 and 6 change the page's mood the most. Mix-and-match is possible (e.g. 5's record + 1's status line).
